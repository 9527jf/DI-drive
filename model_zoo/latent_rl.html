Latent Reinforcement Learning

Latent Reinforcement Learning is a category of policy settings which generate latent representations that can conpletely characterize the features related to the task in the input observation. Techically, it uses a Variational Autoencoder (VAE) to get latent embeddings, and use it to train RL policies.

Our implementation refers to Model-free RL for AD with DI-drive and DI-engine and transfers it to more general cases. In the following documents, it will be explained detailly.
